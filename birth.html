What to Expect When You Go into Labor

Below are the 3 main stages of labor:

  1. Dilation
    2. Click Here for More Details
    1. Early Labor
      • Cervix dilates from 0 to 4 cm
      • Lasts 6-12+ hours
      • End of this phase marked by the 5-1-1 rule: Contractions are 5 minutes apart, each lasting 1 minute, for at least 1 hour
    2. Active Labor
      • Cervix dilates from 5-10 cm
      • Water "breaks"
      • Lasts 4-8 hours
    3. Transition
      • Contractions happen on top of each other, usually very intense
      • Lasts 30 minutes to an hour

  2. Birth
    3. Click Here for More Details
    1. Resting
      • Uterus stops contracting for 10-20 minutes
      • Doesn't occur in all labors
    2. Descent
      • Baby is facing mother's back, and is head-down
      • Baby begins its final descent out of the vaginal canal
    3. Crowning and Birth
      • Vagina stretches to its maximum circumference, also known as the "Ring of Fire"
      • The entire 2nd stage of labor takes 20 minutes to 2+ hours

  3. Placental Delivery
    4. Click Here for More Details
    • Contractions continue and help push the placenta out of the uterus
    • Lasts a few minutes to 30 minutes
